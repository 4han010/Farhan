Thermoelectric Generator

This was a lab project for the course "Thermodynamics II. We had to construct an electric vehicle as shown above. The car is powered by a thermoelectric generator and a small DC motor. A thermoelectric generator (TEG), also known as a Seebeck generator, is a solid-state device that utilises the Seebeck effect to transform heat flow (temperature differential) into electrical energy. We used tea light as the hot source and ice as the cold one. Our instructor requested that the automobile ascend a 20 degree inclined ramp so that he could assess the vehicle and this vehicle was successful in doing that. We also had to determine the thermodynamic effectiveness of our system and document the car design.
